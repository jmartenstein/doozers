# User-Centered Design (UCD) Principles

User-Centered Design (UCD) is an iterative design process in which designers focus on the users and their needs in each phase of the design process.

## Core Principles

- **Focus on the People:** Understand the needs, preferences, and limitations of end-users.
- **Solve the Right Problem:** Conduct thorough research to define the actual problem before designing solutions.
- **Systemic Thinking:** Recognize that changing one part of a system affects others.
- **Iterative Process:** Design requires continuous cycles of development and testing.
- **Holistic Experience:** Address the whole user experience, including tasks, environments, and emotional impact.
- **Multi-disciplinary Collaboration:** Involve diverse experts and stakeholders.

## The UCD Workflow (4 Phases)

1. **Understand Context of Use:** Research how and where users will interact with the system.
2. **Specify Requirements:** Identify user pain points and define specific needs.
3. **Design Solutions:** Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes.
4. **Evaluate against Requirements:** Test solutions with real users to assess performance.

## Best Practices for Developers

- **Involve Users Early:** Use feedback as an early-warning system.
- **Use Personas:** Maintain focus on specific user behaviors and needs.
- **Empathy and Ethics:** Respect privacy and quality of life in design.
- **Long-term Monitoring:** Monitor product use over time to maintain effectiveness.
